# 07 - ASO & Keywords Strategy: Avada Return & Exchange

## 1. Shopify App Store Search Landscape

### How Shopify App Store Search Works

Shopify App Store uses a proprietary algorithm that considers:
- **App name** (30 characters max) -- highest keyword weight
- **App card subtitle** (62 characters max) -- second highest weight
- **App description** (key introduction: first 100 characters shown in search)
- **Category classification** -- determines which category pages the app appears on
- **Install velocity** -- recent install rate signals relevance
- **Rating and review count** -- higher ratings boost ranking
- **Engagement signals** -- conversion rate from listing view to install

The two most important ranking factors are: (1) keyword placement in title/subtitle, and (2) install velocity combined with rating.

### Category Landscape

The primary category is: **Orders and Shipping > Returns and Warranty > Returns and Exchanges**

As of April 2026, this category contains 122+ apps with the following distribution:
- 4.9+ rated: ~30 apps
- 4.7-4.8 rated: ~15 apps
- 4.0-4.6 rated: ~20 apps
- Below 4.0: ~10 apps
- Newer/unrated: ~47 apps

The top 10 apps by category ranking all have 100+ reviews and 4.7+ ratings. Breaking into the top 10 requires both high rating (4.8+) and strong install velocity.

## 4. Recommended App Name and Listing Strategy

### App Name Options (30 characters max)

| Option | Characters | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|--------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **Avada Returns & Exchanges** | 27 | Brand + primary keywords, follows market pattern | Generic pattern, less memorable |
| **Avada Return & Exchange** | 25 | Slightly shorter, singular form | Less keyword coverage (singular vs. plural) |
| **Avada Returns: Smart Exchange** | 31 | Emphasizes exchange + smart/AI angle | Over character limit |
| **Avada EasyReturns** | 17 | Short, memorable, implies simplicity | Misses "exchange" keyword |
| **Avada Returns & Exchange Pro** | 30 | Adds "Pro" quality signal | "Pro" adds no search value |

**Recommendation**: **"Avada Returns & Exchanges"** (27 characters)

This follows the established market pattern that merchants recognize, includes both primary keywords ("returns" and "exchanges"), and leads with the Avada brand name for ecosystem recognition. The plural "Exchanges" matches how 9 of 11 top competitors format their title.

### App Card Subtitle (62 characters max)

| Option | Characters | Keywords Hit |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| "Free returns portal with exchanges, store credit & auto-approve" | 65 | Over limit |
| "Automate returns, exchanges, refunds & labels. 50 free/mo" | 58 | returns, exchanges, refunds, labels, free |
| "Free return portal. Exchange, refund, store credit & labels" | 60 | return, exchange, refund, store credit, labels |
| "Manage returns & exchanges with free portal, labels & refunds" | 62 | returns, exchanges, portal, labels, refunds |
| "50 free returns/mo. Exchanges, store credit, labels & tracking" | 63 | Over limit |
| "Automate returns, exchanges & refunds. 50 free returns/mo" | 57 | returns, exchanges, refunds, free |

**Recommendation**: **"Automate returns, exchanges & refunds. 50 free returns/mo"** (57 characters)

This hits 4 primary keywords (returns, exchanges, refunds, free), communicates the key differentiator (50 free returns), and uses the action verb "Automate" that resonates with merchants seeking efficiency. The "50 free" callout is unique -- no other app highlights free volume in their subtitle.

### Key Introduction (First 100 characters)

**Recommendation**:
> "The most generous free returns app for Shopify. 50 free returns/month with exchanges and store credit."

This immediately communicates: (1) free, (2) generous, (3) specific quantity, (4) exchanges, (5) store credit. This is designed to stop merchants from scrolling past and clicking into the listing.

### Full Description Keyword Strategy

The app description should be structured to maximize keyword density while remaining readable:

**Section 1: Problem + Solution (Keywords: returns, exchange, refund, Shopify)**
- Open with the pain point: "Tired of losing revenue to refunds?"
- Position Avada as the solution with specific benefits

**Section 2: Key Features List (Keywords: portal, labels, tracking, automation, store credit)**
- Bullet-point format for scannability
- Each bullet starts with a keyword-rich action phrase

**Section 3: Pricing Highlight (Keywords: free, affordable, no hidden fees)**
- Call out the 50 free returns prominently
- Compare implicitly to competitors without naming them

**Section 4: Why Choose Avada (Keywords: instant exchange, fraud detection, analytics)**
- Differentiation features as keyword-rich paragraphs
- Social proof and trust signals

**Section 5: Integration & Support (Keywords: Shopify Flow, Gorgias, Klaviyo)**
- Integration ecosystem keywords for merchants searching by tool name

### Target Keyword Density in Description

| Keyword | Target Appearances | Natural Placement |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| returns | 8-12 | Throughout all sections |
| exchange(s) | 6-10 | Features, pricing, differentiation |
| refund | 4-6 | Problem statement, features |
| store credit | 3-5 | Features, pricing |

## 5. App Store Listing Optimization Strategy

### Visual Assets Strategy

**App Icon**:
- Use the Avada brand colors with a return/exchange visual metaphor (circular arrows)
- Must be recognizable at small sizes in search results
- Clean, modern design matching Polaris aesthetic

**Featured Image / Hero Banner**:
- Primary message: "50 Free Returns/Month"
- Secondary message: "Exchanges, Store Credit, Auto-Approve"
- Show a clean UI screenshot of the return portal
- Use social proof: "Rated 4.9/5 by 500+ merchants" (target)

**Screenshots (5-8 recommended)**:
1. Customer-facing return portal (branded, clean)
2. Admin dashboard with return requests
3. Exchange flow (variant swap in action)
4. Analytics dashboard showing return insights
5. Quick-start wizard (10-minute setup)
6. Email notification templates
7. Policy configuration interface
8. Mobile-responsive return portal

**Demo Video (30-60 seconds)**:
- Show the 10-minute setup flow
- Demonstrate a customer initiating a return
- Show the exchange flow
- End with pricing (50 free returns/month)

### Review and Rating Strategy

**Target**: 4.9+ rating with 100+ reviews within first 3 months

**Tactics**:
1. **In-app review prompt**: After a merchant successfully processes their 10th return, prompt for a review with a direct link to Shopify App Store
2. **Onboarding follow-up**: Send a personal email 7 days after install asking about experience and linking to review
3. **Feature milestone prompts**: When a merchant first uses exchange, store credit, or analytics, celebrate the moment and invite review
4. **Support-to-review pipeline**: After resolving a support ticket, ask satisfied merchants to share their experience
5. **Early adopter program**: Invite first 50 merchants to be "founding reviewers" with early access to P1 features

**Review response strategy**:
- Respond to every review within 24 hours
- For negative reviews: acknowledge, fix, follow up publicly with resolution
- For positive reviews: thank and highlight specific features they mentioned

### Category Optimization

**Primary category**: Orders and Shipping > Returns and Warranty > Returns and Exchanges
**Secondary category** (if available): Orders and Shipping > Returns and Warranty > Other (for warranty/claims features in future)

Ensure the app appears in related search categories:
- "Store credit" searches
- "Refund management" searches
- "Order management" related searches

## 6. Competitor Listing Weaknesses to Exploit

### Weakness 1: No Competitor Highlights Free Volume in Subtitle

Every competitor's subtitle focuses on features or outcomes, but none call out their free tier volume. "50 free returns/mo" in our subtitle immediately differentiates in search results.

### Weakness 2: Generic Descriptions

Most competitors use generic marketing language in their first 100 characters. Specific numbers ("50 free returns," "10-minute setup," "91% cheaper than Loop") will stand out.

### Weakness 3: Poor Screenshot Quality

Several mid-tier competitors (Rich Returns, ReturnZap, Yanet) have dated or low-quality screenshots. Professional, Polaris-aligned screenshots will signal quality.

### Weakness 4: No Video Demos

Most competitors lack demo videos or have outdated ones. A high-quality 30-60 second demo showing the setup wizard and customer experience will significantly boost conversion.

### Weakness 5: Slow Review Response

Many competitors have unaddressed 1-star reviews from months ago. Rapid, empathetic review responses signal active development and merchant care.

## 9. ASO Monitoring and Iteration

### Metrics to Track Weekly

| Metric | Tool | Action Threshold |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| Category ranking position | Shopify Partner Dashboard | Drop below target = investigate |
| Search impressions by keyword | Shopify Partner Dashboard | Low impressions = optimize keywords |
| Listing page conversion rate | Shopify Partner Dashboard | Below 30% = redesign screenshots/description |
| Install velocity (daily/weekly) | Shopify Partner Dashboard | Declining = boost promotion |
| Review count and rating | Shopify App Store | Below 4.8 = address negative reviews urgently |
| Competitor ranking changes | Manual check | Competitor rises = analyze their changes |

### Monthly Optimization Cycle

1. **Review keyword performance**: Which search terms drive the most installs?
2. **A/B test descriptions**: Try different opening lines and feature emphasis
3. **Update screenshots**: Reflect new features and seasonal themes
4. **Analyze competitor listings**: Has anyone changed their positioning?
5. **Content gap analysis**: What keywords are we not ranking for yet?
6. **Review sentiment analysis**: What do merchants praise? Emphasize in listing.
